## üîç Project Overview  
This project investigates how **climate change**, **gentrification**, and **housing insecurity** intersect for low-income seniors living in **Barrio Logan**, one of San Diego‚Äôs most culturally significant yet environmentally vulnerable neighborhoods.

Using a multi-method approach including **GIS spatial analysis**, **demographic enrichment**, **heat exposure modeling**, **stakeholder mapping**, **SWOT analysis**, and a **business model canvas**, the project develops a framework for a **climate-resilient senior housing cooperative**.

The goal is to highlight:
- Where climate risks and displacement pressures overlap  
- Which populations are most affected  
- What design and policy features support resilient, community-led housing  
- How a cooperative model can protect seniors while preserving cultural identity  

---

## üìä Key Insights  
- Barrio Logan‚Äôs **Heat Severity Index = 1.4**, but seniors face heightened vulnerability due to inadequate cooling infrastructure and economic constraints.  
- **382 seniors** live within the planning area, many in older housing stock with limited insulation or tree canopy.  
- **247 households** are severely rent-burdened, amplifying displacement risk.  
- Several **city-owned parcels** present real opportunities for climate-resilient development.  
- Community organizations (Environmental Health Coalition, nonprofits, local artists, residents) show strong support for **cooperative, land-trust, and resilience-focused models**.

## üß© Business Model Summary  
This project proposes a **community-led, climate-resilient senior cooperative** featuring:

- Passive cooling design  
- Solar power + microgrid-ready infrastructure  
- Shared green space + mobility improvements  
- Bilingual (English/Spanish) community governance  
- Partnerships with city agencies, nonprofits, and senior housing advocates
