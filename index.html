Project Overview

This project examines how climate risk, aging, and housing insecurity intersect in Barrio Logan, a historically marginalized and culturally rich neighborhood facing extreme heat and displacement pressures.

Using open data, GIS, and community-centered frameworks, the project proposes a climate-resilient senior housing cooperative that aligns with: SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities), SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities & Communities), and SDG 13 (Climate Action).

Key Questions

  • Where do climate risks and senior vulnerability overlap in Barrio Logan?
  • How many seniors and households are currently at risk of heat and displacement?
  • What city-owned parcels could support resilient senior housing?
  • How can a cooperative model protect seniors while preserving cultural identity?

Methods & Tools

  • Spatial Analysis: ArcGIS Online, zonal statistics, parcel screening
  • Demographics: ACS enrichment, age structure, rent burden
  • Risk Indicators: heat severity, senior density, displacement risk
  • Strategy: SWOT analysis, stakeholder mapping, business model canvas

Governance & Stakeholders

The housing cooperative model combines municipal partners, community organizations, and residents to co-govern a climate-resilient senior housing development.

Stakeholder Perspectives

  • Seniors: safety, affordability, cultural continuity, access to care.
  • City & Agencies: climate action goals, housing targets, equity.
  • Community Orgs: anti-displacement, environmental justice, advocacy.
  • Developers / Partners: funding models, feasibility, long-term operations.

Strategic Framing

  • SWOT Analysis: strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats of a cooperative model in Barrio Logan.
  • PowerInterest Grid: identifies who must be closely engaged vs. kept informed.
  • Business Model Canvas: value proposition, key partners, cost structure, and revenue streams for a climate-resilient senior co-op.
